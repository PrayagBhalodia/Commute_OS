# Transport Mode Comparison

## Road modes
Cabs offer door-to-door convenience and luggage handling but are exposed to traffic and surge pricing. Buses can be economical but may have fixed stops and variable travel time. Autos are useful for short urban trips where permitted.

## Rail and metro
Trains can be efficient for intercity corridors and may offer more usable space than flights, but station access and booking status matter. Metros are predictable in congested cities but need first-mile and last-mile planning.

## Flights
Flights are fastest over long distances in the air, but total journey time includes airport transfers, check-in, security, boarding, baggage reclaim, and disruption risk. Compare door-to-door duration rather than flight duration alone. Prices and availability must come from live or simulated booking tools, never RAG.

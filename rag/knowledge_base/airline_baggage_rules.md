# Airline Baggage Rules

## Cabin baggage
Domestic airline cabin allowances vary by operator and fare. A common baseline is one cabin bag plus a small personal item, but the traveller must verify the allowance shown by the operating carrier. Security restrictions apply to liquids, sharp objects, batteries, and other dangerous goods. Medicines and assistive devices may have separate rules.

## Checked baggage
Checked baggage allowance depends on airline, route, cabin, and fare family. Excess weight or additional pieces may incur charges at the operator's current rate. DMOS policy information is guidance only and must never be treated as live inventory or a live fee quote. Travellers should identify oversized, fragile, sporting, or valuable items before booking.

## Connections
For separate tickets, baggage may need to be collected and checked again. The itinerary should include enough time for baggage reclaim, terminal transfer, security, and check-in. International connections can require customs or immigration even when the final destination is elsewhere.

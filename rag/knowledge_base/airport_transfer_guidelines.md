# Airport Transfer Guidelines

## Arrival buffer
For domestic flights, plan enough time for terminal entry, bag drop, security, and walking to the gate. A two-hour airport arrival buffer is a conservative default when conditions are unknown. International departures generally need a longer buffer. Always follow the operating airline's current check-in deadline.

## Ground transfer
Road travel to an airport is sensitive to traffic, weather, terminal access, and event congestion. Add contingency time when travel crosses a city or occurs during peak periods. Confirm the exact terminal before departure because changing terminals can require an additional road or shuttle transfer.

## Connections
Self-transfers and separate tickets need more time than protected connections. The traveller may need to reclaim baggage, change terminals, and repeat security. DMOS should explain these risks and never imply that a connection is guaranteed.
